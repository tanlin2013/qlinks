qlinks.open_sys.lindbladian.build_liouvillian(H, jumps, *, fmt='csc', dtype=<class 'numpy.complex128'>)[source]#

Build the sparse Lindbladian superoperator L such that

d/dt vec(rho) = L @ vec(rho)

for the master equation

dot(rho) = -i[H, rho]
  • sum_mu (L_mu rho L_mu^dagger

    • 1/2 {L_mu^dagger L_mu, rho})

Parameters:

  • H (sparse matrix, shape (d, d)) – Hermitian Hamiltonian.

  • jumps (list of sparse matrices, each shape (d, d)) – Jump operators.

  • fmt (str) – Sparse format of the returned matrix.

  • dtype (dtype) – Complex dtype.

Returns:

Lio – Sparse Liouvillian in vectorized form.

Return type:

sparse matrix, shape (d^2, d^2)

Time-evolve rho via RK4 in vectorized Liouville space.

Parameters:

  • Lio (sparse matrix, shape (d^2, d^2)) – Liouvillian.

  • times (1D ndarray) – Time grid.

  • renormalize_trace (bool) – Optionally enforce Tr(rho)=1 after each step.

  • enforce_hermiticity (bool) – Optionally symmetrize rho after each step.

rhos – Density matrices at the requested times.

qlinks.open_sys.lindbladian.evolve_liouvillian_krylov(rho0, Lio, times)[source]#
Time-evolve rho via expm_multiply:

vec(rho(t)) = exp(Lio * t) vec(rho0)

This is often the best sparse “Krylov-like” approach in SciPy.

Parameters:

  • rho0 (ndarray, shape (d, d)) – Initial density matrix.

  • Lio (sparse matrix, shape (d^2, d^2)) – Liouvillian.

  • times (1D ndarray) – Times at which rho(t) is returned.

Returns:

rhos – Density matrices at the requested times.

Return type:

list of ndarray
